ABOUT

Partners in Excellence

Where

Tier-1 elementary schools in the Nashua School District. 80% of the students attending Tier-1 schools are on a meal plan. There are 5 Tier-1 elementary schools in Nashua.

Current Problem

There are too many varying levels of capabilities in a class. Also, the curriculum covered is very broad and does not focus on just mastering few skills, the syllabus seems to favor quantity over quality.

Our Solution

Work with underperforming students once a week for 90 minutes. The ratio of students to volunteers is 3 to 1 as we try to separate the kids based on their level. We focus on mastering 1 topic every week.

Session Layout

We break the year into three, 9 week, sessions; Math, Science, and First Lego League

The hour and a half class is split into 3 parts. We spend 30 minutes helping the kids with their school homework. Next, we spend roughly 20 minutes teaching that weeks topic. Lastly, we spend the rest of the class playing a game or doing an experiment that relates to the topic.

The most important part of STEMBuds is using children's natural curiosity and love for games to plant a genuine interest in learning new topics.

How do the kids perform?

At the end of the 2016-2017 school year we gave a test with the same topics and the average score was 86 percent. 32 kids got a perfect score!
